*Spellbound* was my final project for the course *Game Design Theory and Practice*. For *Advanced Studio*, I am working on a remake of the original *Spellbound* made in Godot. This version features a completely rebuilt environment, characters and third person perspective, as well as a new spellcasting system and new art assets. <br/><br/>
*Spellbound* is a third person rouge-like where the player-- playing as a wizard's aprentice-- must battle the origami creatures that have come to life from the pages of spellbooks within the wizard's tower. With each creature defeated, the player collects paper for their spellbook, which they can use to learn a new spell. With these new spells, the player can progress through the levels to challange bigger and bigger creatures kept higher in the tower. At this stage, *Spellbound* features a variety of spells, each with their own unique effects, as well as a few simple enemies and an introductory first floor of 4 levels, ending with a single boss encounter. <br/><br/>
The development process has consisted of rebuilding the game from the ground up, with a focus on improving the gameplay systems with **[Object Oriented Programming Techniques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Object-oriented_programming)**, updating art assets with Blender, and creating a more refined 3D environment. The spellcasting system has also been redesigned to be more intuitive and engaging for the player. The build pipeline has changed slightly, as Godot 4.6 has brought more animation capabilities to the engine. This enables animation to be done directly in Godot rather than in Blender, leaving less room for import errors and tighter control over the characters. <br/><br/>
*Spellbound* is an importaint project for me. It was the first game I had ever produced, and at the time the group that was working on it had very little experience with game development. The game was a great learning experience, and being given the chance to remake it with the knowledge and skills I have gained in years since 2022 is a great opportunity for me. Being able to see the improvements I have made as a game developer through the remake process is very rewarding, but beyond that, I want to create the game that we envisioned when we first began creating concepts for the *Game Design* course. I want to create a game that I can be proud of, and I believe that the remake of *Spellbound* will allow me to achieve that goal.
